Year-End Self-Assessment

FY 2025–26 (April 2025 – March 2026)

Overview

Over the past fiscal year, I led the end-to-end experience design across Wiom's two core verticals — Customer (Demand side) and Partner (Supply side) — shaping the product experience across 3 apps: the Customer App, the Partner App, and the newly launched Wiom Expert App for technicians.

Across 12 months, I consistently delivered design work every sprint cycle, contributing to 50+ feature releases spanning new product launches, behavioural nudge systems, incentive programs, operational tooling, payment innovations, and a full app revamp. Every deliverable was shipped within the expected sprint timelines.

My work directly influenced key business levers — installation completion rates, partner behaviour and retention, customer onboarding conversion, fraud prevention, and the company's strategic pivot to a flexible recharge model (Recharge Wala Ghar Ka Net).

A. Partner / Supply Side Achievements

1. Full Page Notification (FPN) System — Iterative Evolution ▼

Designed and iterated on the Full Page Notification system across 4 major versions (Apr '25 – Nov '25), which is the primary decision-making interface for partners when a new connection arrives.

  • V1 (Apr): Introduced FPN with connection details to help partners make informed accept/reject decisions. Designed rejection reason capture for actionable ops data.
  • V2 (May): Shifted focus to a map-based view showing the lead's position relative to existing customers — giving proximity and density value.
  • V3 (Aug): Redesigned to surface the single most actionable data point — distance to nearest existing installation. Added cancel-prevention prompts.
  • V4 (Sep–Nov): Improved transition experience, handled fallback states, and ensured robustness across edge cases.
Impact: FPN became the primary lever for partner acceptance rates. Each iteration was driven by observed partner behaviour — moving from information overload to focused, actionable prompts that reduced decision friction and improved booking acceptance.

2. Rohit Lottery Program — V1 → V3 ▼

Designed the end-to-end lottery experience across 3 major versions over 6 months (Jun '25 – Nov '25), evolving it from a simple reward into a sophisticated behavioural nudge system.

  • V1 (Jun): Introduced lottery rewards with every successful task — the first gamification layer in the partner ecosystem.
  • V1.5 (Jul): Added stacking incentives — partners earn more for completing 2 installations/day. Designed prompts to drive the "2-per-day" behaviour shift.
  • V2 (Aug): Introduced variable reward mechanics and eligibility criteria tied to installation quality (optical power range) and customer satisfaction (rating ≥ 3/5).
  • V3 (Nov): Introduced the traffic-light state system (Green/Yellow/Red) giving partners real-time visibility into their eligibility status.
Impact: The lottery evolved from a flat reward into a multi-dimensional behavioural engine driving installation volume, quality compliance, and customer satisfaction. The traffic-light system shifted the dynamic from "random reward" to "earned reward."

3. Wiom Expert App — Empowering Technicians ▼

Designed and launched a brand-new standalone app for technicians (May '25), separating their workflow from the partner app.

  • Launch (May): Core task management interface enabling technicians to manage assignments independently.
  • Wallet (Jun): Dedicated wallet so technicians receive payments directly, with bank transfer functionality.
  • Language Support (Sep): Multi-language capability with app-restart language switching.
Impact: The Expert App gave technicians professional identity and direct financial empowerment. By decoupling their workflow, it reduced dependency bottlenecks and improved task completion autonomy.

4. Installation Quality & Process Enforcement ▼

Designed a progressive system of quality gates and process improvements across the installation journey (Aug '25 – Jan '26).

  • OCS Improvement (Aug): Redesigned the device pickup checklist experience for better compliance.
  • Out-of-Range Education & Blocking (Oct): Introduced education flows first, then hard blocks after threshold — balancing enforcement with learning.
  • Slot-Based System (Oct → Jan): Replaced old SLA model — 4 tickets/day to accept, 2 installs/day. Evolved into the new SLA.
  • 3-Plug Installation & Photo Capture (Jan): New hardware step and upfront documentation.
Impact: Created a layered quality enforcement system — education first, then guardrails, then hard enforcement. This respected the partner relationship while systematically raising installation standards.

5. ISP Recharge Automation (H8) — V1 to Agentic ▼

Designed the end-to-end partner experience for automating ISP portal recharges across 3 versions (Aug '25 – Feb '26).

  • V1 (Aug): First introduction of H8 automation — reducing manual portal interaction.
  • V2 (Dec): Added live preview of H8 portal during transaction — transparency and confidence.
  • V3 / Agentic (Feb): Fully agentic flow where the system autonomously handles portal interaction.
Impact: Progressively eliminated the most tedious operational task — manual ISP portal recharges. Moved from semi-automated to fully agentic, saving significant daily time and reducing errors.

6. Partner Education & Trust Building ▼

  • Incentive Visibility (Jun): Made the incentive structure transparent.
  • Pathshala (Nov): In-app education module for features, rules, and policies.
  • Upcoming Changes (Dec): Proactive communication before rule changes.
  • "Wiom Earns When You Earn" (Dec): Shared-purpose messaging.
  • Contract & Education (Jan): Partner contract experience and education flows.
  • PayG Education & Quiz (Feb–Mar): Preparation for the PayG model shift.
Impact: Built a culture of proactive education rather than reactive enforcement — improving compliance and reducing support escalations.

7. Operational & Platform Improvements ▼

  • Single Device Restriction (Aug): One partner app per phone number — preventing misuse.
  • Device Inventory (Sep–Nov): Scan-and-add devices; new proof requirements.
  • Language Change (Sep): Multi-language support across Partner and Expert apps.
  • Account Frozen Communication (Sep): Clear communication flows.
  • Partner Location Capture (Jan): Address capture for future logistics.
  • Nomenclature Update (Feb): Terminology consistency across all flows.
Impact: Collectively reduced operational friction, prevented fraud, and created a more reliable platform experience for thousands of partners.

8. New CSP App (Mar '26) ▼

Designed a completely new CSP/Partner App built on a new OS-based architecture — a foundational reset for the partner platform.

Impact: Strategic investment in the next generation of Wiom's partner tooling, designed for scalability and a modern experience framework.

B. Customer / Demand Side Achievements

1. Booking & Onboarding Journey ▼

Redesigned the customer booking and onboarding experience across multiple iterations (Apr '25 – Dec '25).

  • New Booking Flow (Apr): Fresh journey from scratch with improved information architecture.
  • Post-Booking Experience (May, Aug): Iteratively improved awaiting screen, reducing customer anxiety.
  • Real Images (Jul): Replaced illustrations with photographs — increasing trust for Bharat users.
  • Rs. 25 Anxiety Resolution (Jul): Addressed booking fee hesitation through reassuring communication.
  • Installation Expectations (Oct, Dec): Clear expectations about scheduling and partner call confirmation.
Impact: The booking-to-installation journey became significantly smoother. By addressing specific anxiety points at each stage, we reduced drop-offs and improved the first-impression experience.

2. PayG / Recharge Wala Ghar Ka Net — Strategic Pivot ▼

Led the design of Wiom's most significant product shift — from fixed 28-day plans to flexible Pay-As-You-Go recharges (Oct '25 – Mar '26).

  • Concept Introduction (Oct): First experiment with small plans (1, 2, 10 days) for a controlled group.
  • Diwali Offer (Nov): Packaged PayG as a festive offer for controlled rollout.
  • Use-Case Education (Dec): Handholding experiences showing real flexible recharge use cases.
  • Full Pivot (Jan): Core offering — Rs. 100 booking + Rs. 300 installation, recharge for any number of days.
  • Migration (Feb): Migration experience for existing customers to the new PayG model.
Impact: The single most impactful initiative of the year. PayG fundamentally changed Wiom's value proposition from "another broadband provider" to "flexible, affordable internet for Bharat." The phased rollout ensured validation at every step.

3. Chat-Based App Revamp (Jan '26) ▼

Designed a complete reimagination of the customer app — moving from traditional screens to a conversational, chat-based interaction model.

  • Everything — from booking to recharge to support — now happens through chat.
  • Designed typing experience, message flows, and interaction patterns from the ground up.
  • Continued iterating through Feb–Mar based on user feedback.
Impact: A bold design bet that simplified the entire customer journey into a single, familiar interaction pattern. For Bharat users comfortable with WhatsApp, this dramatically lowered the learning curve.

4. Payment & Plan Experience ▼

  • Custom Checkout (Jun): Built custom checkout for more control during payments.
  • Autopay Fix (Sep): Resolved multiple payment issues in autopay flow.
  • Plan Selection Experiments (Sep): Tested 3 different variations of plan discovery and selection.
  • Non-Delight to Delight Migration (Nov): Upgrade path for customers moving to better plans.
Impact: Payment is the highest-friction moment. Each improvement directly reduced drop-offs and support tickets around payment confusion and failed transactions.

5. Service, Support & Device Recovery ▼

  • Refund on Missed Installation (Jun): Clean, trust-preserving refund experience.
  • Improved Service Tickets (Oct): Reopen existing tickets and create new ticket types.
  • Router Recovery V1 (Dec): Self-service device return with refund option.
  • Router Recovery V2 — Handshake (Mar): Proper refund handshake on device return.
Impact: Even in failure scenarios, the experience remains respectful and transparent — critical for word-of-mouth in Bharat markets where trust is community-driven.

6. Trust, Security & Retention ▼

  • Login Restriction (Sep): 3 phone numbers per device — preventing fraud.
  • PlayStore Review (Nov): In-app flow to capture reviews alongside ratings.
  • Wiom TV Cross-Promotion (Oct): Post-booking upsell nudges for controlled segments.
  • Mobile Number Update (Jul): Self-service number update from within the app.
Impact: Improved platform integrity, customer trust, and organic growth through better app store presence.

C. Timeline Performance

  • Delivered design work consistently every month for 12 consecutive months — no missed sprint cycles.
  • Managed parallel workstreams across Customer App, Partner App, and Expert App simultaneously.
  • Design hand-offs completed on schedule, enabling development teams to maintain sprint velocity.
  • Adapted quickly to strategic pivots (PayG model, chat-based revamp) without delivery gaps.
  • Every major initiative delivered within its planned timeline, with iterative improvements in subsequent sprints.
